What implications does the lack of electricity have?

Education

● Less than two-thirds of children complete primary school. ● A staggering 90% of students attend schools without electricity. ● Reliable electricity would allow for extended learning times, enabling classes to start earlier and end later. ● Electricity enables the use of digital tools and resources, such as computers and the internet, which are vital for a modern and more effective education. ● Students can benefit from up-to-date textbooks, interactive learning modules, and educational videos, often unavailable in non-electrified classrooms. ● Better education is widely recognized as a crucial factor in reducing poverty. ● Improved educational outcomes lead to better living standards for the people of Sub-Saharan Africa.

Health

● Nearly 80,000 young children unintentionally ingest kerosene from lamps every year, leading to hospitalization and medical treatment. ● Alarmingly, 60% of these cases result in chemically induced pneumonia, a preventable condition if safer lighting options were available through access to electricity. ● The use of kerosene lamps and traditional biomass for cooking exposes families to harmful indoor air pollution.b It's the leading cause of respiratory infections, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and lung cancer. ● Without electricity, healthcare facilities struggle to provide essential services. ● Many clinics lack refrigeration for vaccines and medicines, reliable lighting for night-time emergencies, and the power needed for essential medical equipment. ●The lack of electricity hampers the delivery of quality healthcare and increases the risk of disease outbreaks and poor health outcomes.

Closing the Energy Gap in Africa

Africa, with its vast solar resources, receives an average of 2000 kWh of solar radiance annually, making it an ideal location for solar energy production. Here's why solar farms are a promising renewable energy solution for Africa:

Abundant Energy Source: Solar farms harness the sun's energy, which is plentiful and sustainable. Environmentally Friendly: They produce no emissions or pollution during operation, making them a green energy choice. Scalability: Solar farms can be easily scaled, allowing for gradual expansion from small to large installations. Low Maintenance: They require minimal upkeep and operate silently, adding to their practicality. Land Availability: While solar farms need significant land, this is less of an issue in Africa. In 2022, the population density in Sub-Saharan Africa was approximately 48.3 inhabitants per square kilometer, indicating ample space for solar installations.

Investing in solar power can significantly reduce the energy gap between Africa and other continents, leveraging the continent's natural advantages to create a sustainable and prosperous future. What would it look like if we utilize this untapped energy?
